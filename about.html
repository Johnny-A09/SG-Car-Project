About Us

A living encyclopedia of cars — their stories, specs, and cultural footprints.

Our story — why we started AutoArchive

AutoArchive began as a conversation between two friends in a garage — one loved torque curves, the other loved the stories behind the steel. They realized there was no single place that treated cars as both machines and cultural artifacts. So they built AutoArchive: part reference, part storytelling workshop, and all heart.

Where knowledge meets passion

We combine verified specs (engine options, production years, trim levels) with human stories: designer interviews, ownership tales, and the cultural moments that made a model iconic. Our aim is to be useful to enthusiasts, restorers, students, and casual readers alike.

How we work

Articles are researched from manufacturer material, historical archives, and community contributions. Every entry includes a source list when available, and we encourage corrections and additions — cars are living subjects and our pages evolve as we do.

Milestones

  • Year 1: Launched with 200 model pages and a small community forum.
  • Year 2: Added high-resolution spec sheets and photo galleries.
  • Ongoing: Expanding to include restoration guides, buyer tips, and multimedia interviews.

Our tone & promise

We write clearly, cite responsibly, and avoid romanticizing unsafe or illegal behavior. Accuracy matters; when we’re unsure about a fact we say so and invite sources.

Values

Accuracy
We verify facts and cite sources where possible.
Accessibility
Technical details explained so anyone can understand.
Community
Built with contributions from owners and experts.
Respect
We honor creators, manufacturers, and the people behind the stories.
